# InScape DMCA Policy

**Effective Date: May 19, 2024**

InScape (“we,” “us,” or “our”) respects intellectual property rights and complies with the U.S. Digital Millennium Copyright Act (“DMCA”). This DMCA Policy outlines our procedures for addressing copyright infringement claims related to content on our websites, mobile applications, and related services (collectively, the “Services”). As a B2B2C platform hosting Partner advertisements, we provide this process to protect copyright holders and maintain our safe harbor status under the DMCA.



## 1. Submitting a DMCA Notice
If you believe content on our Services infringes your copyright, submit a written DMCA notice to our Designated Agent with the following:
- A physical or electronic signature of the copyright owner or their authorized agent.
- Identification of the copyrighted work claimed to be infringed (e.g., title, description, or URL).
- Identification of the infringing material, including its location on our Services (e.g., URL or screenshot).
- Your contact information (name, address, phone number, email).
- A statement that you have a good-faith belief the use is not authorized by the copyright owner, their agent, or law.
- A statement, under penalty of perjury, that the information in your notice is accurate and you are the copyright owner or authorized to act on their behalf.

Incomplete notices may not be processed. Email notices to dmca@inscape.com are preferred for faster handling.

## 2. Our Response to DMCA Notices
Upon receiving a valid DMCA notice, we will:
- Promptly remove or disable access to the allegedly infringing content.
- Notify the content provider (e.g., Partner or user) of the removal and provide your notice, if applicable.
- Document the action to maintain our safe harbor status.

We aim to process notices within 7 business days, though complex cases may take longer.

## 3. Counter-Notice Procedure
If you believe content was wrongly removed due to a DMCA notice, you may submit a counter-notice to our Designated Agent with:
- Your physical or electronic signature.
- Identification of the removed content and its prior location on the Services.
- A statement, under penalty of perjury, that you have a good-faith belief the content was removed in error.
- Your contact information (name, address, phone number, email).
- Consent to the jurisdiction of the courts in Melbourne, Victoria, Australia, and agreement to accept service of process from the original complainant.

Send counter-notices to dmca@inscape.com. If valid, we may restore the content within 10-14 business days, unless the complainant notifies us of legal action.

## 4. Repeat Infringer Policy
InScape terminates accounts of users or Partners identified as repeat infringers, defined as those with multiple confirmed DMCA violations. We reserve the right to suspend or remove content at our discretion to protect our Services.

## 5. Jurisdiction
This DMCA Policy is governed by U.S. federal law (17 U.S.C. § 512) for the purposes of processing copyright infringement claims and maintaining safe harbor status. Any ancillary disputes arising from this policy (e.g., claims related to false notices or counter-notices) are governed by the laws of Victoria, Australia, and subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts in Melbourne, Victoria, Australia, unless mandatory local laws require otherwise (e.g., consumer protections in your jurisdiction). This aligns with InScape’s Terms and Conditions (Sections 12.5 and 18.1).

## 6. False Notices
Submitting false DMCA notices or counter-notices may result in legal liability. InScape may share your information with the affected party if a notice is deemed abusive.

## 7. Changes to This Policy
We may update this DMCA Policy to reflect legal or operational changes. Updates will be posted on our website with a revised effective date.
